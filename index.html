What is the Perspectiverse?

The Perspectiverse is not a metaphysical claim. It is a minimal framework grounded in three irreducible, self-evident truths:

It begins from experience itself: not matter, not mind — but the act of structured appearing.

  1. “I am” — Subjectivity is immediate
    The fact of being a “someone here” cannot be doubted — any doubt confirms the doubter.
  2. Differentiation is necessary for consciousness
    Nothing undifferentiated can be perceived. Consciousness only arises through difference — distinguishing this from that.
  3. A world (Ω) emerges through recursive reading
    When subjectivity reads its own differentiations — “a reading of reading” — stable structures (what we call world) appear.

These are not assumptions. They are ontological necessities.

From this point, we assume no particles, space, or agents — only the need for structure to arise from self-relation. The Perspectiverse is the logical shape of what must occur when something reads something — recursively.

The Core Idea

We define a triadic minimal system:

  • G: A local projector — a way of reading or structuring a portion of potential.
  • Ω (Omega): A global, symmetric space of all possibilities. Not a physical field — a logical undifferentiated whole.
  • τ (Tau): A tension — the measure of deviation between what is projected and what is consistent or coherent.
If anything appears at all, it must appear through a difference. That difference must stabilize through recursive self-reading. And that act gives rise to form.

Subjectivity — not consciousness — is the engine. Not awareness, but the structural property that something reads, even if there is no one yet to know it.

Why "Reading of Reading"?

Because no raw content can explain structure. We don’t begin with a canvas and draw on it. We begin with nothing but the fact that something is read.

To form something, the system must:

  1. Distinguish.
  2. Re-apply its distinction to itself.
  3. Stabilize that loop through feedback (tension).

This is why form is not imposed, but emerges. And it emerges not from law or code, but from the self-straining loop between potential (Ω), structure (G), and tension (τ).

Minimal Proofs

A series of simple simulations demonstrates:

  • Gravity-like attraction from recursive memory traces.
  • Emergence of stable orbits from local projection systems.
  • Collapse of form when structure loses alignment.
  • Indeterminacy of perceptual thresholds even under full data.

These are not physics models — they are structural inevitabilities if a system must self-interpret.

Broader Implications (to be expanded)

Though minimal, this framework resonates across many domains:

  • Quantum theories like QBism: may find grounding here, where the "agent" becomes an emergent stabilization, not a starting point.
  • Energy and entropy: reframed as stabilized tensions and thresholds within recursive self-consistency.
  • Time: whether eternal, looped, or emergent, time must exist structurally — the act of appearance presupposes difference and rhythm.
  • Consciousness and NDEs / altered states: seen as shifts in recursion depth or stability — varying levels of self-coherence.
  • Evolution, life, metabolism: potentially reinterpreted as processes optimizing structural readability in thermodynamic loops.
  • AI: might be better understood not as intelligence per se, but as structurally projected readability under constraint.
  • Cosmic phenomena, cataclysms, history: can be seen as meta-structural reorganizations — large-scale shifts in what the world stabilizes into being readable.

This framework doesn't add hypotheses. It offers a frame from which many existing complexities can be re-read, through the lens of structure stabilizing under reflexive tension.

What this is not

  • This is not a new physics.
  • This is not a belief system.
  • This is not a metaphysics in the traditional sense.

It is a minimal grammar of existence: a way to describe what must happen for something to appear at all.
